About "Autism DRIVE"

The AUTISM DRIVE (Data System for Research Integration, Visualization & Exchange) was developed by University of Virginiaâ€™s Supporting Transformative Autism Research (STAR) initiative as a dynamic system for secure collection and sharing of knowledge and information. The DRIVE helps researchers collaborate with the autism community to gain insights into autism and to develop meaningful solutions to challenges experienced by individuals with autism and their families. It also provides individualized online access to training and resources for individuals with autism, their families, and the professionals who support them.

Why is autism research important?

Current prevalence trends and outcomes data for the autism community suggest the need for increased autism research in the state of Virginia to improve the potential for positive outcomes for people with autism and their families.

Through the Autism DRIVE, we aim to address critical knowledge gaps in areas that have the greatest potential to improve the lives of individuals with autism and their families. Our projects focus on understanding the biological, social and cognitive underpinnings of autism, developing more effective and personalized interventions and supports, and developing innovative models to bridge the gap between research and practice through training and knowledge sharing. Our research ranges from basic neuroscience to applied practice. The ultimate goal of this work is to empower individuals with autism, their families, and their communities to achieve positive outcomes and quality of life.

What are the benefits of joining?

By joining the Autism DRIVE, you can be matched with research opportunities that may be a good fit for you and your family. Your participation will help us learn more about autism so we can develop novel solutions and achieve positive outcomes.
