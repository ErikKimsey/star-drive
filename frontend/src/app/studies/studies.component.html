About our research

The Supporting Transformative Autism Research (STAR) project was designed to ignite discovery, improve systems of care, and equip communities with best practices to support people with autism across the lifespan. STAR brings together researchers, community partners, individuals with autism and their families to identify challenges and co-develop innovative solutions.

We aim to address critical knowledge gaps in areas that have the greatest potential to improve the lives of individuals with autism and their families. Our projects focus on understanding the biological, social and cognitive underpinnings of autism, developing more effective and personalized interventions and supports, and developing innovative models to bridge the gap between research and practice through training and knowledge sharing. Our research ranges from basic neuroscience to applied practice. The ultimate goal of this work is to empower individuals with autism, their families, and their communities to achieve positive outcomes and quality of life.
